About MUN
It is an extracurricular activity in which students typically role play delegates to the United Nations and simulate UN councils. The delegates represent different countries and attempt to solve real world issues using the policies and perspectives of their assigned country. At the end of the year, we hold a conference to allow our delegates the chance and experience to implement what they've learned throughout the year. Model United Nations, which involves substantial researching, public speaking, debating, and writing skills, as well as critical thinking, teamwork, and leadership abilities. Students learn about the world as they prepare for MUN conferences, represent countries other than their own, and present possible solutions to global problems in councils.
About STEM Obour MUN
is a branch of MUN in Obour STEM School, it is our first season, We aim to set up a conference which is originally an educational simulation of the United Nations. Students act as representatives of a country, organization or person and must solve a problem with other delegates from around the world. This conference teaches participants research, speaking, debate and writing skills, as well as critical thinking, teamwork and leadership abilities. Delegates conduct research prior to conferences, and must draft position papers and develop policy proposals that they will discuss with other delegates on their committee. At the end of the conference, delegates will vote on written policies, called draft resolutions, with the aim of passing them by a majority
Mission
Introduce students to the MUN program that promotes diversity, respect for human dignity and rights, concern for the common good, in addition to permitting students to diplomatically resolve issues at the forefront of international relations. Furthermore, we aim to familiarize these students with what NDU stands for and prepare them to be future leaders who can exercise reason upon knowledge and shape a world of truth, justice, love, and freedom.
Vision
Ensure that the students of today are the effective and efficient leaders of tomorrow, with developed interpersonal, public speaking, writing, and research skills. Additionally, we envision a future where all participating students have high levels of self-esteem and self-confidence, which are qualities that can help them widen their circle of friends and in their future endeavors, and ultimately shape their path to success,teach young people to solve problems through diplomacy, discussion and respect for the opinions.
